Learn about flight delays

Delay's probable between 11pm-3am

Based on RITA flight delay information for US domestic flights in 2008, bar chart below let's you explore the relationship between average departure delay time during different time periods.

The average flight delay between US domestic flights is 9 minutes. However, this is simple average calculation and cannot be trusted always.


Average flight delays by hour of day (minutes)

*Note: Red bars indicate delays of more than 40 minutes.



It is evident that passangers wait longer on flights scheduled to depart between 11pm to 3am. This graph provides a quick overview of the potentially best times to fly during any day of the week. (Howver, at 5-6am there is negative time delay).

Next is an interesting correlation...

Number of flights by hour of day

*Note: Red bars indicate number of flights less than 50,000.



From the chart above, average flight delays appear to be inversely related to the volume of flights happening in a given hour. It can be observed that, hours with the highest number of flights also have the lowest average delay times. There seems to be one exception though, at 4am there is a relatively low volume of flights and a low average delay time. This pattern may be attributed to the fact of having less number of support staff during odd operating hours.

Next, let's check out how the schedule goes during holidays...

Average flight delays by month (minutes)

*Note: Red dots indicate delays of more than 9 minutes (the average)


December, having the holiday season clearly stands out as the month with longest average flight delay, over 17 minutes. But the difference between the best and worst months is just 13 minutes, while the difference between the best and worst hour of day is a staggaring 130 minutes

This analysis can help us conclude to avoid booking flights between delay prone hours of the day i.e. 11pm-3am.
